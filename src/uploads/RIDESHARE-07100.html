Why Uber Is Being Sued — The Reality Behind the Uber Lawsuit

For years, Uber prioritized rapid growth over passenger safety. Internal documents revealed at trial show Uber was aware of driver assault risks as early as 2014 — yet reports of sexual assault, harassment, and misconduct were routinely downplayed or dismissed.

Survivors who filed complaints found them ignored. Drivers with prior misconduct remained on the platform. The background check systems designed to prevent exactly these situations were either absent or dangerously inadequate.

The Uber lawsuit exposes how the company failed to implement basic safety protocols — proper screening, real-time monitoring, and responsive complaint systems — that could have prevented thousands of incidents.
